Research
The flight of insects cannot be explained by aerodynamic theories relying on quasi-steady flow. Insect wings generate substantially higher lift than those predicted by quasi-steady theories thereby necessitating reseach that take into consideration the mechanisms of unsteady aerodynamics. Numerical investigations undertaken in the lab using the immersed boundary method have explored several aspects of unsteady aerodynamics acting on flapping wings. Immersed boundary method is an attractive technique for simulations involving moving boundaries as it removes the need for re-meshing.
